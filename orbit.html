L2 Orbit

Webb orbits the Sun–Earth L2 Lagrange point, about 1.5 million kilometers from Earth. This position keeps the telescope cold, stable, and in constant communication with the ground.

What is L2?

The second Lagrange point (L2) is one of five Lagrange points in the Sun–Earth system. At L2, the combined gravitational pull of the Sun and Earth allows a spacecraft to orbit the Sun with the same period as Earth while staying in a fixed geometry relative to both. L2 lies on the line from the Sun through Earth, about 1.5 million km beyond Earth (roughly four times the Moon’s distance).

The five Sun–Earth Lagrange points. L2 is on the far side of Earth from the Sun. Credit: NASA (adapted)

Why L2 for Webb?

Webb observes in infrared. To detect faint heat from distant galaxies and cool objects, the telescope and instruments must be extremely cold. At L2, Webb can keep its tennis-court-sized sunshield pointed at both the Sun and Earth, blocking their heat and light. The telescope stays in shadow, and the instruments can cool to roughly 40 K (−388 °F) or below. No other orbit around Earth would allow this combination of cold, stability, and clear view of the sky.

L2 also gives Webb a wide, uninterrupted view of the universe. The telescope can observe any target for long periods without Earth or the Moon blocking the line of sight.

Halo orbit

Webb does not sit exactly at the L2 point. It travels in a halo orbit around L2—a looping path that keeps the spacecraft near L2 while avoiding the shadow of Earth. This orbit is roughly the size of the Moon’s orbit around Earth. Small thruster burns (station-keeping) are done about every three weeks to maintain the halo orbit. Fuel for these maneuvers limits the mission lifetime to an expected 20+ years.

Distance and communication

Webb is about 1.5 million km (roughly 1 million miles) from Earth. Radio signals take about five seconds to travel one way. All commands, telemetry, and science data are sent through the Deep Space Network (DSN). Webb can store large amounts of data on board and downlink when a DSN antenna is available.
