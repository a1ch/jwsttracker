Technical Specifications

Key numbers and design details for the James Webb Space Telescope.

Optics

Primary mirror 6.5 m diameter, 18 hexagonal segments, beryllium, gold-coated. Collecting area ~25 m².
Secondary mirror Convex hyperbola, ~0.74 m diameter, held by three struts.
Mirror folding Two wing sections fold back for launch; deployed in space.
Wavelength range 0.6 µm (visible/near-IR) to 28 µm (mid-infrared).
Webb’s optics deliver sharp, deep images across the infrared. Credit: NASA, ESA, CSA, STScI

Sunshield

Size Roughly tennis court–sized when deployed (~21 m × 14 m).
Layers 5 layers of Kapton with coatings to reflect and radiate heat.
Function Keeps Sun and Earth on one side so the telescope and instruments stay in shadow and cold.

Orbit and mission

Orbit Sun–Earth L2 Lagrange point, halo orbit. ~1.5 million km from Earth.
Launch vehicle Ariane 5 ECA (ESA), December 25, 2021.
Design lifetime Minimum 5 years; goal 10+ years. Fuel could allow 20+ years.

Instruments

NIRCam Near-Infrared Camera. 0.6–5 µm. Imaging, wavefront sensing, coronagraphy.
NIRSpec Near-Infrared Spectrograph. 0.6–5.3 µm. Multi-object, fixed-slit, IFU spectroscopy.
MIRI Mid-Infrared Instrument. 5–28 µm. Imaging and spectroscopy. Cryocooled to ~7 K.
NIRISS Near-Infrared Imager and Slitless Spectrograph. 0.8–5 µm. Imaging, WFSS, SOSS, AMI.
FGS Fine Guidance Sensor. Pointing stability; optional imaging mode.

Partners

NASA leads the mission and provides the telescope, sunshield, and several instrument components. ESA (European Space Agency) provides the Ariane 5 launch, NIRSpec, and part of MIRI. CSA (Canadian Space Agency) provides the FGS and NIRISS. Science operations are led by the Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI) in Baltimore.
