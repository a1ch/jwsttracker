Webb’s iconic first images

These images were among the first full-color science images released by the James Webb Space Telescope in July 2022 and have become some of the most famous views of the universe.

Webb’s First Deep Field — Galaxy cluster SMACS 0723. Thousands of galaxies in a tiny patch of sky; some of the faintest are from the first billion years of the universe.
Pillars of Creation — Star-forming region in the Eagle Nebula (M16). Credit: NASA, ESA, CSA, STScI
Stephan’s Quintet — Five galaxies, four of which are locked in a close dance. A huge NIRCam mosaic showing gas, dust, and star formation.
Sombrero Galaxy (M104) — Combined NIRCam and MIRI view. Near-infrared shows stars; mid-infrared reveals dust and cooler material.

More context

Webb’s First Deep Field revealed galaxies that existed when the universe was only a few hundred million years old. The Pillars of Creation show how radiation from massive stars blows bubbles and shapes the gas where new stars form. Stephan’s Quintet helps astronomers study how galaxies interact and trigger star formation. The Sombrero Galaxy demonstrates how combining NIRCam and MIRI gives a fuller picture of stars and dust.
